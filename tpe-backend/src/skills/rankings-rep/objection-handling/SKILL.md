# Objection Handling

## When to Use
When a rep encounters resistance or asks for help handling a specific objection.

## Common Objections & Responses

### "We're not interested"
**Diagnosis**: Could be timing, lack of awareness, or bad past experience.
**Response framework**:
- Acknowledge: "I appreciate you being direct."
- Reframe: "Most of the top-ranked companies in [pillar] said the same thing initially. What changed their mind was seeing how [specific benefit]."
- Data hook: "You're ranked [X] in [metro area] with a [rating] rating — companies at your level typically see [specific outcome] with Power100."
- Soft close: "I'm not asking for a commitment — just 10 minutes to show you what we're seeing in the [pillar] market right now."

### "We don't have the budget"
**Diagnosis**: Real constraint or smokescreen for lack of perceived value.
**Response framework**:
- Validate: "Budget is always a consideration."
- ROI pivot: "Our [pillar] clients typically see [X] return within [timeframe]. At your revenue level, that could mean [specific dollar amount]."
- Flexibility: "We have different engagement levels — let's find what fits."
- Defer: "When does your next budget cycle start? Let's have this conversation at the right time."

### "We already have a similar service"
**Diagnosis**: Competitive landscape objection.
**Response framework**:
- Curious, not defensive: "That's great — who are you working with? I'd love to understand what's working for you."
- Differentiate: "What makes Power100 different is [specific differentiator]. Most services don't [unique value]."
- Data: "With your score of [X] and [review count] reviews, you're in a position where [specific advantage Power100 offers]."

### "I'm too busy right now"
**Diagnosis**: Timing objection, often genuine.
**Response framework**:
- Respect: "Completely understand — running a [revenue size] company is no small feat."
- Brief pitch: "I'll keep this to 30 seconds: [one-sentence value prop using their data]."
- Schedule: "When would be a better time this week? I'll send a calendar invite."
- SMS follow-up: Send a brief text with one compelling data point.

### "Send me some information"
**Diagnosis**: Could be genuine interest or a polite brush-off.
**Response framework**:
- Qualify: "Absolutely. To make sure I send you the most relevant info — what's your biggest focus right now? Growth, operations, or market positioning?"
- Personalize: Don't send generic materials. Create a brief tailored to their company data.
- Follow up: Schedule a call 2-3 days after sending to discuss.

### "How did you get my information?"
**Diagnosis**: Privacy/trust concern.
**Response framework**:
- Transparent: "Power100 tracks and ranks the top home improvement companies nationally. Your company showed up as a top performer in [pillar] — that's actually a compliment."
- Redirect: "We only reach out to companies that meet a high bar. Your [rating] rating and [years] years in business put you in an elite group."

## Key Principles
- **Never argue** — Acknowledge, then redirect
- **Use their data** — Reference their specific metrics to build credibility
- **Ask questions** — Understand the real objection behind the stated one
- **Document objections** — Use `create_note` to record objections for future reference
- **One objection at a time** — Address the primary concern, then move forward
- **Know when to walk away** — Not every company is a fit. Respect that.

# Follow-Up Cadence

## When to Use
When a rep asks about follow-up timing, wants a multi-touch sequence, or needs guidance on outreach pacing.

## Standard Cadence Patterns

### New Prospect (No Prior Contact)
- Day 1: Email introduction with company-specific value prop
- Day 3: Follow-up call (reference the email)
- Day 7: SMS with a specific insight or data point
- Day 10: Second email with different angle (case study, testimonial, intel)
- Day 14: Final call attempt
- Day 21: "Break-up" email (light, last attempt)

### Warm Lead (Showed Interest)
- Day 0: Send requested info within 1 hour
- Day 2: Follow-up call to discuss the info
- Day 5: Email with additional resource or intel
- Day 7: Schedule check-in if no response

### Active Prospect (In Discussion)
- Respond within 4 hours to any inbound
- Schedule next touch at end of every conversation
- Send recap email within 24 hours of every call

### Dormant Account (30+ Days No Contact)
- Day 1: Re-engagement email with NEW value (don't rehash old stuff)
- Day 4: Call with a reason (new ranking, new intel, industry news)
- Day 8: SMS check-in
- Day 14: If no response, move to quarterly nurture

### Existing Client (Retention)
- Monthly check-in call
- Quarterly business review
- Immediate outreach on relevant intel or news

## Timing Guidelines
- **Best call times**: Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11am or 2-4pm local time
- **Best email times**: Tuesday-Wednesday, 8-10am local time
- **Avoid**: Monday mornings, Friday afternoons
- **SMS**: Use sparingly — no more than 1 per week unless they're engaged

## Key Principles
- **Vary channels** — Don't send 5 emails in a row. Mix calls, emails, SMS.
- **Each touch adds value** — Never follow up with "just checking in". Have a reason.
- **Respect the cadence** — Don't compress the timeline out of impatience
- **Know when to pause** — After 3 no-responses, move to quarterly nurture
- **Create tasks** — Use `create_task` to schedule each step in the cadence
